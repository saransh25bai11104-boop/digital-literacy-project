# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Task 4 – Part B**

---

## DO's ✅

1. **Keep your profile photo professional.** Use a clear, decent photo — especially on LinkedIn. First impressions matter online just as much as in person.

2. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: would you be comfortable if your professor, parent, or a future employer saw this? If not, don't post it.

3. **Use privacy settings.** Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal details. Limit public access to sensitive content.

4. **Give credit when sharing.** Always mention the original source when sharing someone else's article, photo, or idea. This shows respect and avoids plagiarism.

5. **Engage positively and constructively.** Comment respectfully, even when you disagree. Healthy discussions are fine; personal attacks are never acceptable.

6. **Keep your accounts secure.** Use strong, unique passwords for each platform and enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.

7. **Separate personal and professional profiles.** Use LinkedIn and GitHub for academic and professional content. Keep casual content (if any) on separate, private accounts.

---

## DON'Ts ❌

1. **Don't share personal details publicly.** Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, Aadhaar number, or daily routine on any public platform.

2. **Don't engage in cyberbullying.** Never mock, threaten, or harass others online. It is both ethically wrong and legally punishable in India under the IT Act.

3. **Don't spread unverified information.** Always check if news or claims are from a credible source before sharing. Spreading misinformation can cause real harm.

4. **Don't post complaints about your college, professors, or classmates.** Such posts can damage your academic record and future career prospects.

5. **Don't click suspicious links.** Links in DMs or comment sections may lead to phishing sites that steal your passwords or install malware.

6. **Don't overshare about your location.** Posting your real-time location or check-ins frequently can compromise your safety.

7. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers.** Especially on platforms like Facebook and Instagram, unknown contacts may have malicious intent.
