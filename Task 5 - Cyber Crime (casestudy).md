# Case Study: Phishing Attack Targeting College Students

**CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Task 5 – Part A**
**Cybercrime Type: Phishing**

---

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which an attacker disguises themselves as a
trustworthy entity — such as a bank, government body, or educational institution — to
trick victims into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or banking
credentials.

---

## Realistic Scenario

Rohan, a first-year engineering student, received an email that appeared to come from
his university's official portal. The email had the university's logo and said:
*"Your student account will be deactivated in 24 hours due to incomplete KYC
verification. Click here to update your details immediately."*

Feeling anxious, Rohan clicked the link. The page looked exactly like the university
login portal. He entered his username, password, and even his Aadhaar number as
requested. Within minutes, the attacker had his credentials and used them to access
his email account, find linked banking details, and attempt a fraudulent UPI transfer.

---

## How It Typically Happens — Step by Step

1. The attacker creates a fake but convincing email mimicking a trusted source.
2. The email creates urgency (e.g., "Act in 24 hours or lose access").
3. The victim clicks the link and lands on a fake website.
4. The victim enters their login credentials or personal information.
5. The attacker captures the data and uses it for identity theft or financial fraud.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- Students and young adults unfamiliar with cyber threats
- People who use the same password across multiple platforms
- Anyone expecting communications from banks, universities, or government portals

---

## Consequences

- Loss of access to email and college accounts
- Financial fraud through UPI or net banking
- Identity theft — attacker may misuse Aadhaar or PAN details
- Emotional distress and difficulty recovering compromised accounts
