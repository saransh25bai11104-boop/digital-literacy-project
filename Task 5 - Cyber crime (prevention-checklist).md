# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist

**CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Task 5 – Part B**
**For College Students in India**

---

## General Cybersecurity

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A good password is at least 12
   characters long and includes uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
   Example: `V!tBhop@l2024` is stronger than `vitbhopal123`.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email,
   GitHub, LinkedIn, and college portal. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA
   adds a second layer of protection.

3. **Never click links in unexpected emails or SMS messages.** If you receive an
   email claiming to be from your bank or college asking you to "verify" your
   account, go directly to the official website instead.

4. **Keep your devices updated.** Install software and operating system updates
   regularly — they often contain critical security patches.

5. **Use secure Wi-Fi only.** Avoid using public Wi-Fi (at cafes, malls, or bus
   stands) for logging into any accounts. If necessary, use a VPN.

6. **Lock your devices.** Always set a PIN or password on your phone and laptop.
   Enable auto-lock after 30–60 seconds of inactivity.

---

## Financial and UPI Safety (Specific to India)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not even with someone
   claiming to be from your bank or NPCI. Legitimate organisations never ask for
   these details over phone or chat.

8. **Verify before paying.** Before sending money via UPI, double-check the
   recipient's UPI ID and phone number. Fraudsters sometimes create fake IDs that
   look almost identical to real ones (e.g., `sbi.support@upi` vs `sbisupport@upi`).

---

## Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you are a victim of any cybercrime — phishing, UPI fraud, identity theft,
cyberbullying, or ransomware — report it immediately:

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 (available 24x7, toll-free)
- **Local Police Cyber Cell:** Visit your nearest police station and ask for the
  Cyber Crime Cell

> **Important:** Report as early as possible. In financial fraud cases, early
> reporting increases the chance of recovering your money.
